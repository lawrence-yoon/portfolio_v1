About Me

Hello, my name is Lawrence Yoon. I am a self taught Software Developer.

I was born and raised in the five boroughs of New York City, and was educated through the public school system. Upon graduating from Stuyvesant High School, I set my focus toward studying Mechanical Engineering at SUNY University at Buffalo. One reason why I had chose this discipline was to better understand the physical world. I wanted to understand the "hows" and "whys" of man-made inventions, from buildings to engines. After graduating with a bachelors of science, I went to work, and wore a lot of different hats. From handyman, to CAD tech, and finally engineer for the NYC Department of Environmental Protection.

I've learned a lot from the NYCDEP, and it was fulfilling work, as I was actively contributing to the community by working to improve city infrastructure. But eventually I started to feel discontented. Some manual processes had to be done on a regular basis, and the mundane repetition started to wear me down. I started to look into workarounds, such as writing code to automate certain tasks. But due to security reasons, I was not allowed to install any unapproved software on the company computers.

I decided to take time off for some much needed self reflection. After a lot of introspection, I'd decided to pursue a career in Software Development. While I still valued my time spent in Mechanical Engineering, I knew that a main part of the discontent that I had felt was from the feeling of lagging behind in technology. Mechanical engineering will always be tried and true, and will never become obsolete. But it is also an old dog that doesn't learn new tricks.

The allure of learning new, developing technologies was too appealing, and after dabbling in Python and realizing the power of coding, I've decided that I must pursue this newfound passion. The instant gratification of battling troublesome code for hours and finally getting it to run has got to be one of the best feelings in the world.
